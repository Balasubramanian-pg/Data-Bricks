## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 62. collect Action and Risks exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The purpose of 62. collect Action and Risks is to provide a systematic approach to identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks associated with a particular action or set of actions. This process is essential in various domains, including business, healthcare, and finance, where informed decision-making is critical. The class of problems it addresses includes risk management, decision-making, and strategic planning.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The conceptual overview of 62. collect Action and Risks involves a cyclical process that includes identifying potential actions, assessing associated risks, evaluating the likelihood and impact of these risks, and developing strategies to mitigate or manage them. This process is iterative, with continuous monitoring and review to ensure that risks are adequately addressed and actions are adjusted as necessary.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Action | A specific task, decision, or activity undertaken to achieve a particular goal or objective. |
| Risk | The possibility of an undesirable event or outcome occurring as a result of a particular action or set of actions. |
| Risk Assessment | The process of identifying, evaluating, and prioritizing risks associated with a particular action or set of actions. |
| Mitigation Strategy | A plan or set of actions designed to reduce the likelihood or impact of a particular risk. |
| Residual Risk | The risk that remains after mitigation strategies have been implemented. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of 62. collect Action and Risks include:
- **Risk Identification**: The process of recognizing potential risks associated with a particular action or set of actions.
- **Risk Evaluation**: The assessment of the likelihood and potential impact of identified risks.
- **Risk Prioritization**: The ranking of risks based on their likelihood and potential impact.
- **Mitigation and Management**: The development and implementation of strategies to reduce or manage risks.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for 62. collect Action and Risks typically involves the following steps:
1. **Action Identification**: Define the action or set of actions to be taken.
2. **Risk Assessment**: Identify, evaluate, and prioritize risks associated with the action(s).
3. **Mitigation Strategy Development**: Develop plans to mitigate or manage identified risks.
4. **Implementation and Monitoring**: Implement mitigation strategies and continuously monitor the effectiveness of these strategies.
5. **Review and Adjustment**: Regularly review the risk landscape and adjust actions and mitigation strategies as necessary.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in 62. collect Action and Risks include:
- **Proactive Risk Management**: Identifying and addressing potential risks before they materialize.
- **Continuous Monitoring**: Regularly reviewing and updating risk assessments and mitigation strategies.
- **Collaborative Approach**: Involving stakeholders from various domains in the risk assessment and mitigation process.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in 62. collect Action and Risks include:
- **Reactive Approach**: Only addressing risks after they have materialized.
- **Insufficient Stakeholder Engagement**: Failing to involve relevant stakeholders in the risk assessment and mitigation process.
- **Inadequate Monitoring**: Failing to regularly review and update risk assessments and mitigation strategies.
