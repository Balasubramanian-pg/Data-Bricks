# 116. Mosaic AI Overview

Canonical documentation for 116. Mosaic AI Overview. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 116. Mosaic AI Overview exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The Mosaic AI Overview is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of artificial intelligence (AI) and its applications in various domains. It addresses the need for a unified framework to integrate multiple AI technologies, such as machine learning, natural language processing, and computer vision, to solve complex problems. The primary problem space includes developing intelligent systems that can learn, reason, and interact with humans and other systems.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
The Mosaic AI Overview presents a modular and hierarchical structure, comprising multiple layers and components. The foundation layer consists of data ingestion, processing, and storage. The middle layer includes machine learning, deep learning, and other AI algorithms. The top layer comprises application-specific modules, such as natural language processing, computer vision, and decision support systems. This modular architecture enables the integration of various AI technologies and facilitates the development of customized solutions.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Artificial Intelligence (AI) | The development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, and problem-solving. |
| Machine Learning (ML) | A subset of AI that involves training algorithms on data to enable predictive modeling and decision-making. |
| Deep Learning (DL) | A type of ML that uses neural networks with multiple layers to analyze complex data, such as images, speech, and text. |
| Natural Language Processing (NLP) | A field of AI that focuses on the interaction between computers and humans in natural language, including language understanding, generation, and processing. |
| Computer Vision | A field of AI that enables computers to interpret and understand visual data from images and videos. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of the Mosaic AI Overview include:
* **Modularity**: The ability to integrate multiple AI technologies and modules to create customized solutions.
* **Hierarchical structure**: The organization of AI components into layers, enabling efficient data flow and processing.
* **Data-driven decision-making**: The use of data analytics and AI algorithms to inform decision-making and drive business outcomes.
* **Human-AI collaboration**: The design of AI systems that can interact with humans and other systems to achieve common goals.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for the Mosaic AI Overview consists of the following components:
1. **Data ingestion**: Collecting and processing data from various sources.
2. **Data processing**: Cleaning, transforming, and storing data for analysis.
3. **Machine learning**: Training AI algorithms on data to enable predictive modeling and decision-making.
4. **Application development**: Building customized applications using AI technologies and modules.
5. **Deployment and maintenance**: Deploying AI solutions and ensuring their ongoing maintenance and updates.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in the Mosaic AI Overview include:
* **Microservices architecture**: Breaking down AI applications into smaller, independent services that can be developed, deployed, and maintained separately.
* **Containerization**: Using containers to package and deploy AI applications, ensuring consistency and portability across different environments.
* **Cloud-based deployment**: Deploying AI solutions on cloud platforms to enable scalability, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in the Mosaic AI Overview include:
* **Monolithic architecture**: Developing AI applications as large, monolithic systems that are difficult to maintain and update.
* **Insufficient testing**: Failing to thoroughly test AI applications, leading to errors, biases, and poor performance.
* **Lack of transparency**: Failing to provide clear explanations and interpretations of AI-driven decisions and outcomes.
