# 23. Reusable Gold Models

Canonical documentation for 23. Reusable Gold Models. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 23. Reusable Gold Models exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The primary purpose of Reusable Gold Models is to provide a standardized framework for creating, managing, and utilizing gold models in various contexts, such as data science, machine learning, and software development. The problem space addressed by Reusable Gold Models includes the lack of standardization, reusability, and maintainability of gold models, leading to duplicated effort, inconsistencies, and decreased productivity.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Reusable Gold Models can be conceptualized as a hierarchical structure consisting of abstract representations of gold models, which are then instantiated and customized for specific use cases. This structure enables the creation of a library of reusable gold models that can be easily adapted, combined, and extended to accommodate diverse requirements and applications.

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of Reusable Gold Models include:
* **Modularity**: Breaking down complex gold models into smaller, independent components that can be easily reused and combined.
* **Abstraction**: Defining gold models at a high level of abstraction, allowing for flexibility and adaptability across different contexts and applications.
* **Standardization**: Establishing common standards, formats, and interfaces for gold models to ensure interoperability and reusability.
* **Versioning**: Managing different versions of gold models, including changes, updates, and revisions, to maintain a clear audit trail and ensure reproducibility.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for Reusable Gold Models consists of the following components:
* **Metadata**: Descriptive information about the gold model, including its purpose, scope, and constraints.
* **Parameters**: Configurable settings and parameters that define the behavior and characteristics of the gold model.
* **Constraints**: Rules and limitations that govern the use and adaptation of the gold model.
* **Interfaces**: Standardized interfaces for interacting with the gold model, including APIs, data formats, and protocols.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns for Reusable Gold Models include:
* **Template-based instantiation**: Creating model instances from predefined templates, customizing parameters and constraints as needed.
* **Model composition**: Combining multiple reusable gold models to create complex, hierarchical models.
* **Model refinement**: Iteratively refining and updating gold models based on feedback, new data, or changing requirements.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns for Reusable Gold Models include:
* **Model duplication**: Creating multiple, identical copies of gold models, leading to maintenance and consistency issues.
* **Model rigidity**: Failing to design gold models with flexibility and adaptability in mind, resulting in limited reusability.
* **Model obsolescence**: Neglecting to update or maintain gold models, causing them to become outdated, irrelevant, or obsolete.
