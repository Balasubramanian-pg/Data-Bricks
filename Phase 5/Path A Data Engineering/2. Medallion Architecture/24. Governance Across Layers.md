# 24. Governance Across Layers

Canonical documentation for 24. Governance Across Layers. This document defines the conceptual model, terminology, and standard usage patterns.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is implementation-agnostic and intended to serve as a stable reference.

## 1. Purpose and Problem Space
Describe why 24. Governance Across Layers exists and the class of problems it addresses.
The primary purpose of Governance Across Layers is to provide a structured approach to managing and governing complex systems, applications, and infrastructures that span multiple layers, including technological, organizational, and social layers. This approach addresses the class of problems related to ensuring consistency, compliance, and coordination across these layers, thereby facilitating effective decision-making, risk management, and resource allocation.

## 2. Conceptual Overview
Provide a high-level mental model of the topic.
Governance Across Layers involves the establishment of a hierarchical framework that integrates governance mechanisms, policies, and procedures across different layers of an organization or system. This framework enables the alignment of objectives, strategies, and operations across these layers, ensuring that governance is effective, efficient, and adaptable to changing circumstances.

## 3. Terminology and Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Governance | The process of establishing and implementing policies, procedures, and standards to ensure effective management and oversight of an organization or system. |
| Layer | A distinct level or stratum within an organization or system, such as technological, organizational, or social layers. |
| Governance Model | A conceptual framework that outlines the structure, roles, and responsibilities for governance within an organization or system. |
| Policy | A statement of intent or principle that guides decision-making and behavior within an organization or system. |
| Procedure | A detailed, step-by-step guide for implementing a policy or achieving a specific objective. |

## 4. Core Concepts
Explain the fundamental ideas that form the basis of this topic.
The core concepts of Governance Across Layers include:
* **Layered Governance**: The application of governance mechanisms and policies across multiple layers of an organization or system.
* **Integrated Governance**: The coordination and alignment of governance mechanisms and policies across different layers to ensure consistency and effectiveness.
* **Adaptive Governance**: The ability of governance mechanisms and policies to adapt to changing circumstances and evolving requirements.
* **Risk-Based Governance**: The identification, assessment, and mitigation of risks across different layers of an organization or system.

## 5. Standard Model
Describe the generally accepted or recommended model.
The standard model for Governance Across Layers involves the establishment of a hierarchical framework that consists of:
* **Strategic Layer**: Defines the overall vision, mission, and objectives of the organization or system.
* **Tactical Layer**: Translates strategic objectives into specific plans and initiatives.
* **Operational Layer**: Implements plans and initiatives, and ensures day-to-day operations.
* **Technological Layer**: Supports the operational layer with technology and infrastructure.

## 6. Common Patterns
Document recurring, accepted patterns.
Common patterns in Governance Across Layers include:
* **Centralized Governance**: A single, centralized governance body or function that oversees governance across all layers.
* **Decentralized Governance**: Governance is distributed across multiple layers, with each layer having its own governance mechanisms and policies.
* **Hybrid Governance**: A combination of centralized and decentralized governance approaches.

## 7. Anti-Patterns
Describe common but discouraged practices.
Anti-patterns in Governance Across Layers include:
* **Lack of Clear Governance**: Unclear or absent governance mechanisms and policies, leading to confusion and inconsistency.
* **Inadequate Risk Management**: Failure to identify, assess, and mitigate risks across different layers.
* **Insufficient Communication**: Poor communication and coordination between layers, leading to misunderstandings and misalignment.
