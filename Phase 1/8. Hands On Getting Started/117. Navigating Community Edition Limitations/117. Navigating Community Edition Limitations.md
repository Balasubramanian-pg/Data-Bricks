# Navigating Community Edition Limitations

Canonical documentation for Navigating Community Edition Limitations. This document defines concepts, terminology, and standard usage.

## Purpose

The purpose of this documentation is to provide guidance on understanding and working within the limitations of community edition software. Community editions often offer a subset of features compared to their commercial counterparts, and navigating these limitations is crucial for effective utilization and to avoid potential pitfalls. This topic exists to address the problem space of maximizing the potential of community edition software while being aware of and mitigating the impact of their limitations.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is intended to be implementation-agnostic and authoritative.

## Core Concepts

Explain the fundamental ideas that make up the topic.

### Community Edition Limitations
Community edition limitations refer to the restrictions or constraints imposed by the software's feature set, performance, or scalability. Understanding these limitations is essential for planning, deployment, and maintenance.

### Feature Prioritization
Feature prioritization involves identifying and ranking the most critical features needed for a project or operation. This concept is crucial when working with community editions, as it helps in maximizing the utility of the available features.

## Standard Model

Describe the generally accepted or recommended model for this topic.

The standard model for navigating community edition limitations involves a structured approach:
1. **Assessment**: Evaluate the community edition's feature set and limitations.
2. **Planning**: Plan the project or operation, taking into account the identified limitations.
3. **Optimization**: Optimize the use of available features to maximize their utility.
4. **Monitoring**: Continuously monitor the performance and scalability of the community edition software.
