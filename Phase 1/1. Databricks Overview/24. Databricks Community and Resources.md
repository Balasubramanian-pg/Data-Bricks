# Databricks Community and Resources

Canonical documentation for Databricks Community and Resources. This document defines concepts, terminology, and standard usage.

## Purpose

The Databricks Community and Resources topic exists to provide a comprehensive overview of the ecosystem surrounding Databricks, a leading data engineering and analytics platform. This topic addresses the problem space of finding, accessing, and leveraging the various resources, communities, and tools available to Databricks users, developers, and administrators. The goal is to empower individuals and organizations to effectively utilize Databricks and its associated resources to drive business value and innovation.

> [!NOTE]
> This documentation is intended to be implementation-agnostic and authoritative.

## Scope

The scope of this topic includes the following concepts and areas:

**In scope:**
* Databricks community engagement and participation
* Official Databricks resources, such as documentation, tutorials, and blogs
* Third-party resources, including books, courses, and conferences
* Databricks-related tools and integrations

**Out of scope:**
* Tool-specific implementations, such as Apache Spark or Azure Databricks
* Vendor-specific behavior, such as Databricks-specific features or pricing
* Competitor or alternative platforms, such as Amazon EMR or Google Cloud Dataproc

## Core Concepts

The Databricks Community and Resources topic is built around the following core concepts:

### Community Engagement
Community engagement refers to the various ways in which individuals and organizations participate in and contribute to the Databricks ecosystem. This includes attending conferences, meetups, and webinars, as well as engaging with online forums, social media, and blogs.

### Official Resources
Official resources are documents, tools, and services provided by Databricks or its authorized partners. These resources include documentation, tutorials, blogs, and support channels.

### Third-Party Resources
Third-party resources are documents, tools, and services created by individuals or organizations outside of Databricks. These resources include books, courses, conferences, and community-driven projects.

## Standard Model

The standard model for Databricks Community and Resources involves the following components:

1. **Community Engagement**: Participate in online forums, attend conferences and meetups, and engage with social media and blogs.
2. **Official Resources**: Utilize Databricks-provided documentation, tutorials, and support channels.
3. **Third-Party Resources**: Leverage books, courses, and community-driven projects to supplement official resources.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Deviations from the standard model should be explicitly documented and justified.

## Common Patterns

The following patterns are commonly observed in the Databricks Community and Resources ecosystem:

* **Self-directed learning**: Individuals use online resources, such as tutorials and blogs, to learn Databricks skills.
* **Community-driven support**: Users participate in online forums and social media to ask questions and share knowledge.
* **Partnership and collaboration**: Organizations partner with Databricks or other companies to develop custom solutions or integrations.

## Anti-Patterns

The following anti-patterns are commonly observed in the Databricks Community and Resources ecosystem:

> [!WARNING]
> These anti-patterns often lead to maintenance or scalability issues.

* **Lack of community engagement**: Failing to participate in online forums, attend conferences, or engage with social media and blogs.
* **Over-reliance on official resources**: Relying solely on Databricks-provided documentation and support channels, without exploring third-party resources or community-driven projects.
* **Insufficient training and education**: Failing to invest in proper training and education for Databricks users and administrators.

## Edge Cases

The following edge cases are relevant to the Databricks Community and Resources ecosystem:

> [!CAUTION]
> Edge cases are frequently overlooked and may cause incorrect assumptions.

* **Databricks version compatibility**: Ensuring that resources and tools are compatible with the specific version of Databricks being used.
* **Regional or language-specific resources**: Accessing resources and support in languages or regions where Databricks is not widely adopted.
* **Custom or proprietary integrations**: Developing custom integrations or using proprietary tools that may not be widely supported or documented.
